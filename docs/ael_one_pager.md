# AEL - AI-Driven Embedded Engineering

**Date:** 2026-03-20
**Status:** Draft Execution Plan
**Reviewed by:** Andrew Li

## What is AEL?

AEL (AI Embedded Lab) is a new way of doing embedded engineering:

- AI generates, runs, and verifies firmware on real hardware
- Humans define goals, not step-by-step procedures
- Development becomes a closed-loop system

---

## The Shift

Traditional model:

`Human -> Write -> Flash -> Debug -> Test -> Repeat`

AEL model:

`AI -> Generate -> Execute -> Observe -> Iterate`

---

## Why Now?

AI has crossed a critical boundary.

> AI is no longer only helpful. It is becoming capable of taking control of execution.

This shift is happening now, as AI systems become capable of:

- planning multi-step tasks
- executing workflows
- analyzing results
- iterating autonomously

---

## What AI Can Already Do

With AEL, AI can:

- generate firmware from high-level goals
- run tests on real hardware
- detect failures and fix them
- iterate until a working result is achieved

In many cases:

> No manual coding is required.

---

## What Changes

- Engineers move from execution to direction
- Debugging becomes experimentation
- Development becomes repeatable and scalable

---

## What Comes Next

- Exploration of design space instead of single solutions
- Externalized engineering knowledge
- Multi-AI collaboration systems
- Increasing autonomy

---

## Reality Check

AI-driven engineering is not perfect yet.

But the key point is:

> The boundary has been crossed.

---

## Final Thought

AEL is not just a better tool.

> It is a shift in how engineering work is executed.
